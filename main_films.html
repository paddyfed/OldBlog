Main / Films

Apart from Star Wars these are my most favourite films and those films that I am looking forward to.

The Matrix

What it's about:

Futuristic tale in which the world in which we inhabit is actually a computer program created to power super robots that control the earth. Neo, a computer hacker, is unplugged from the Matrix and becomes the one who will save the world.

What I think:

From the first five minutes of the film you are taken on a roller-coaster ride in which you're not sure which is real or not. Brilliant photography and moody atmosphere make this a classic.

Favourite scene:

Near the end when Neo fights Agent Smith. 'Your clip is empty'.

Fight Club

What it's about:

Twisted story about an insomniac and his new-found friend who create a secret fighting society which plans to bring the whole world down to the same level.

What I think:

What really makes this film is the twist in the tale near the end. I completely didn't see it coming and it hit me like a lightning bolt. Brilliant. You have to see it twice, although, to really appreciate the film.

Favourite scene:

The moment in the film that you realise the twist. Boom!

Lock, Stock & Two Smoking Barrels

What it's about:

A group of East-end Londoners are being shafted by another group of East-end Londoners. So they steal hash from a third group of East-end Londoners and try to sell it back to a fourth group of East-end Londoners who originally owned the hash, all with hilarious consequences.

What I think:

A strange story that isn't actually that confusing to follow. Has some great scenes and some hilarious commentary and stories throughout.

Favourite scene:

Funniest moment was when the person came running out of the pub on fire and the lads just shrugged it off. A regular occurrence.

Snatch

What it's about:

Many stories converge around the main plot of a Diamond theft gone wrong and a rigged boxing match with a bare-knuckle fighting Gypsy.

What I think:

A lot like Lock, Stock... but much-much better. Faster more clever and definitely one to see.

Favourite scene:

When the three guys went to rob Brick-Top's Bookies.

The Lord of the Rings: The Fellowship of the Ring

What it's about:

Film interpretation of J.R.R. Tolkien's classic in which a ring must be destroyed before it falls into the hands of the Dark Lord Sauron.

What I think:

WOW!!! Probably the best adaptation of The Lord of the Rings that you're likely to find. This is a brilliant movie & fans of the book shouldn't be disappointed.

Favourite scene:

At the very beginning, the story of the rings of power & the last alliance of Men & Elves.
