The Hobbit - J.R.R. Tolkien

Blurb:

Whisked away from his comfortable, unambitious life in his hobbit-hole in Bag End by Gandalf the wizard and a company of dwarves, Bilbo Bagins finds himself caught up in a plot to raid the treasure hoard of Smaug the Magnificent, a large and very dangerous dragon. Although quite reluctant to take part in this quest, Bilbo surprises even himself by his resourcefullness and his skill as a burglar!

What I think:

A bit more of a childrens book than Lord of the Rings but still an enjoyable read. It's quite interesting to hear about Bilbo's tale especially after reading Lord of the Rings.

Asterix the Gaul - Goscinny & Uderzo

Blurb:

The year is 50BC. Gaul is entirly occupied by the Romans. Well, not entirely... One small village of indomitable Gauls still holds out against the invaders. And life is not easy for the Roman legionaries who garrison the fortified camps of Totorum, Aquarium, Laudanum and Compendium...

What I think:

Before Star Wars books came along these were the only books/comics that I read outside of school. Very funny especially now that I'm older and actually get most of the jokes.
